Our neighborhood (and why it matters)

EC is located in the Shaw neighborhood of Washington, D.C. We're proud to serve our government clients from our nation's capital. We're proud to be a part of the revitalization of the LeDroit Park area. And we're proud of the community itself: this place has history.

After the Civil War, Shaw was built by emancipated African Americans who constructed homes, opened businesses and started an independent bank here. Toward the end of the 19th century, the community came of age as a nucleus of black intellectual and cultural life: a magnet for artists, writers and intellectuals like Langston Hughes, Duke Ellington, Alain LeRoy Locke, Ella Fitzgerald, James Brown. We've named our conference rooms to honor them.

When riots erupted after the assassination of Martin Luther King, Jr., Shaw took a downward slide. But thanks to grassroots community renewal projects spearheaded by civic leaders, the decline didn't last.

Today, our neighborhood is a mix of offices, shops, row houses and 19th-century Victorian homes; professors, electricians, doctors and carpenters. It's a blend of families who've been here for generations, and young professionals just starting out.

In short: it's an edgy, urban area whose history runs deep, and whose future is actively unfolding.

We're invested in its future

Community involvement and citizenship are more than just nice words; they require an investment
of time and resources. At EC, we attend civic events, provide jobs to the local community, recruit
interns from Howard University and do our utmost to be a responsible neighbor and good citizen.
